ls -l: lists file permmissions and when they were last edited
	
	first part before first dash is owner privlages
	second part is guest
	third part is public

chmod- give or take away perms
	chmod 777 test.txt would give everyone all perms while 000 would give everyone no perms
	EX: chmod u-w Hello.txt (would take writing perms away from user for hello.txt)














variables:
r- reading
w- writing
x- exicuting
d- directory

u- user
g- groups
o- other people

4-read
2-write
1-execute
0-no perms
